Feature extraction using MobileNetV2 significantly improved performance compared to the baseline CNN.

The model achieved 63.85% accuracy and a macro F1 score of 0.46, representing a substantial improvement over Week 1 (51% accuracy, 0.26 macro F1). This demonstrates the effectiveness of leveraging pretrained ImageNet features.

Performance improved across most classes, particularly:
- bkl: F1 increased from very low values (~0.09) to 0.50
- vasc: improved from 0.00 to 0.53
- overall macro F1 nearly doubled

The model still performs best on the majority class (nv: F1 = 0.80), but minority classes are now better represented.

Confusion between bkl and mel remains a challenge:
- 19.8% of melanoma cases are misclassified as benign keratosis (clinically dangerous)
- However, confusion is more balanced compared to Week 1

Overall, feature extraction provides faster convergence and significantly better generalization than training from scratch, highlighting the importance of transfer learning for medical imaging tasks.
