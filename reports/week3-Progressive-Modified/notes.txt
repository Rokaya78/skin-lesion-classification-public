Progressive unfreezing achieved strong overall performance while maintaining balanced classification across classes.

The model reached 70.60% accuracy and a macro F1 score of 0.61. Compared to earlier experiments, minority classes and clinically important classes such as melanoma showed significant improvement.

The modified progressive schedule prevented aggressive late-stage unfreezing, resulting in more stable training behavior and reduced overfitting risk.

Although overall accuracy was slightly lower than the previous progressive experiment, macro F1 remained high, indicating balanced performance across lesion categories.

The results confirm that controlled adaptation of pretrained features improves medical image classification performance compared to feature extraction alone.
