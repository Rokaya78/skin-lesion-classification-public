Full fine-tuning achieved the highest macro F1 score among all experiments.

The model reached 63.65% accuracy and a macro F1 score of 0.6519, showing strong balanced performance across classes, especially minority lesion categories.

Compared to progressive unfreezing, full fine-tuning improved class balance and minority-class recognition but reduced overall accuracy and melanoma performance.

Training all backbone layers allowed deeper adaptation to the HAM10000 dataset, resulting in strong performance for rare classes such as akiec, bcc, df, and vasc.

The experiment also showed higher risk of overfitting, as training accuracy became significantly higher than validation accuracy during later epochs.

Overall, full fine-tuning provided the best balanced multi-class performance, while progressive unfreezing remained the best overall classifier in terms of accuracy and melanoma safety.
